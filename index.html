Chapter 1

Instrumental Hours

Music without words, built for focus.
These instrumental tracks create space to think, build, and stay grounded during the hours spent quietly bringing ideas to life.

1 Nuvole Bianche Ludovico Einaudi

Song: "Nuvole Bianche" translate to "white clouds" and appears on Einaudi's 2004 album Una Mattina.

Artist: Einaudi is known for a minimalist / contemporary-classical style that blends classical piano with modern repetition and mood.

2 River Flows In You Yiruma

Song: Released on Yiruma's 2001 album First Love and later became famous from being unofficially associated with Twilight by fans.

Artist: Yiruma (Lee Ru-ma) studied in the UK (Purcell School, then King's College London) and became one of the most widely recognized modern piano composers.

3 Chopin: Nocturne Op. 9 No. 2 Daniel Barenboim

Song: Chopin’s Op. 9 Nocturnes were published in 1832 and dedicated to Marie Pleyel; No. 2 became one of his most famous pieces.

Artist: Barenboim made his international piano debut at age 10 (Vienna/Rome, 1952).

4 Moonlight Sonata Beethoven

Song: The “Moonlight” nickname was not Beethoven’s — it’s commonly traced to critic/poet Ludwig Rellstab after Beethoven’s death.

Artist: Beethoven completed the sonata in 1801 and dedicated it to his pupil Countess Giulietta Guicciardi.

5 Piano Sonata No. 14 in C-Sharp Minor Beethoven

Song: The original subtitle is “Sonata quasi una fantasia” (“sonata in the manner of a fantasy”).

Artist: By the late 1830s, “Moonlight Sonata” had become the popular name in publications—even though critics still debate the nickname.

6 Cello Suite No. 1 in G Major Yo-Yo Ma

Song: Bach’s cello suites were treated like “practice studies” for a long time, until cellist Pablo Casals found a worn copy in a second-hand shop at age 13 and later helped revive them.

Artist: Yo-Yo Ma has recorded Bach’s cello suites three times in studio (a rare “lifetime” project for one performer).

7 Für Elise (Celtic Harp) Celtic Harp

Song: “Für Elise” wasn’t published in Beethoven’s lifetime—it was found and published about 40 years after his death, and “Elise” is still debated.

Artist: This version is a harp-style arrangement of the famous bagatelle.

8 Perfect The Piano Guys

Song: The Piano Guys’ “Perfect” is styled as a solo piano take that leans into the song’s 6/8 feel, which makes it extra “sway” friendly.

Artist: Their official concept paired the cover with a La La Land-inspired video/visual.

9 A Thousand Years Christina Perri

Song: “A Thousand Years” was written for The Twilight Saga: Breaking Dawn — Part 1 and later became a huge wedding anthem.

Artist: Christina Perri wrote it after being asked to create a song for the film and wrote it specifically for Edward + Bella’s love story.
